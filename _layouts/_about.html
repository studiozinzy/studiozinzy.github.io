A little context

I was born and raised in the South of the Netherlands, in a complex childhood marked by marching bands, cultural Catholicism, and question marks about who, amid everybody else, I am. You'll find that, on this website, I don't shy away from those question marks.

I came to Utrecht to study the letters, and I enjoyed the seemingly endless learning opportunities that university life provides. I ran an absurdist magazine, worked as a freelance photographer on the side, and spent hours upon hours in dusty libraries copying obscure books as a research assistant.

I earned my undergraduate degree in language and literature with a thesis on focalization in the graphic novel adaptation of a Dutch classic. Narratology, adaptation, and transmediality remain some of my favorite topics.

Before I put my study books on the shelf for good, I spent a year in seminary studying theology at VU University. It taught me volumes on church history, how infinitely weird Scripture really is, and how strong one's back must be to be a queer person studying alongside young, fierce conservatives. I left after a year.
